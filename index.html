Hansel and Gretel lost in Fuerteventura

Hansel and Gretel live with their mothers in a small village in Fuerteventura. Since their financial situation is not very good, the two siblings decide to help by recording TikTok videos, hoping to earn some money and support their families. One afternoon, while they were walking around looking for phone signal to upload their videos, they met a very kind girl who invited them to eat churros and offered them free wifi. At first, everything seemed perfect, but it was actually a trap. When their mothers realized that Hansel and Gretel had not returned home, they immediately called the police. An intense search began across the island. After several hours of worry and fear, the police finally found the children safe and reunited them with their mothers, who hugged them with relief and happiness. From that day on, Hansel and Gretel learned how important it is not to trust strangers and to always tell adults where they are going.

Overthinking in 4K

Before going out to a party, Riley faces a night full of expectations, doubts, and insecurities. Inside her mind, Joy, Anxiety, Anger, Fear, Disgust, and Sadness constantly debate what to do, what could go wrong, and whether she is truly enough just as she is. As she gets ready for the night, she discovers that she doesn’t need to be perfect or have everything under control to enjoy life. Through a fun, relatable, and emotional story, the short film delivers a clear message: every emotion matters and plays an important role in who we are. True confidence comes when we stop seeking approval from others and allow ourselves to embrace experiences, with all their successes, mistakes, and uncertainties. In the end, it’s not about being liked — it’s about living.

Sleeping Beauty

This modern adaptation of Sleeping Beauty tells the story of Aurora, a princess who is tired of the constant pressure to be perfect. Her mother, Queen Eleanor, expects her to always behave like the ideal princess, but Aurora feels that her worth is judged only by her beauty and by meeting everyone else's expectations. When Maleficent arrives at the castle, she reveals that the kingdom's real problem is its obsession with appearances. As a curse, she declares that the pressure on Aurora will become so overwhelming that she will eventually fall asleep. Later, Aurora meets Leo, a young man who truly listens to her and encourages her to be herself. However, the curse comes true, and Aurora falls into a deep sleep. Instead of being awakened by true love's kiss, the story takes a modern turn: Queen Eleanor realizes her mistakes, listens to her daughter for the first time, and understands that happiness matters more than perfection. Through this reconciliation, Aurora awakens, and their relationship is healed. In the end, Aurora and Leo become a couple and help transform the kingdom into a happier place.

Tangled

The short film is about Rapunzel and her friends searching for Pascal after he disappears. Everything begins on a peaceful day, but when Rapunzel realizes that Pascal is missing, she, Flynn and Mother Gothel start looking for him in different places, such as the river, the cave and the bridge. During the search, they help each other and never give up until they discover what happened. The moral of the story is that friendship and teamwork help solve problems and never give up.

Ugly Duckling

“The Ugly Duckling” is an educational short film and it talks about an important social problem that still exists today: racism and discrimination against people who are seen as different. The story is about a little duckling who is rejected by others because of the way he looks. The other ducks laugh at him, leave him alone, and do not accept him. This makes him feel sad and lonely. One day, he decides to leave and look for a place where he can be accepted. During his journey, he discovers who he really is and learns that being different is not something bad. It is something special that should be respected and valued. With this short film, we want to share a message of equality, inclusion, and respect. Our goal is to help people understand the effects of racism and encourage respect for everyone, no matter their origin, appearance, or culture. In a diverse place like the Canary Islands, it is very important to teach values such as empathy, acceptance and respect for differences, helping to create a fairer and kinder society. Because being different makes our world richer and better.

Ceni at the Party

Ceni feels sad because Lucía and Carla are not kind to her and sometimes make fun of her. Her friends Alex and Leo support her, and the Fairy Godmother helps her believe in herself. At Leo’s party, Ceni feels confident and happy. Lucía and Carla apologize for their behavior and learn that bullying can hurt people. In the end, everyone agrees that respect, friendship, and kindness are important.
